## 1. THE COLLAPSE

### The Linear Tax Is Dead

Learn-memorize-apply - the linear, industrial world algorithm. For roughly two centuries, industry and society were optimized around this algorithm. Industrial growth demanded that we be sequential, predictable, measurable. For good reason - it worked. This is how we got here.

Not everyone fitted the frame, though. I didn't anyway. Warriors, navigators, inventors often operated above the linear layer. Dealt with uncertainty, unpredictability and risk as their daily job. Staked their lives for breakthrough and change - labelled broken outside that context.

Then the creative economy hit. What was supposed to be 30 years of liberation became the same painted cage. It did liberate a few. The rest? Same "ten thousand hours" of linear grinding before earning the right to create.

The linear execution was always the tax you had to pay for domain knowledge. The whole system was built around selling it. The linear tax is gone. So is the way you earn it.

Sam Altman, AI Impact Summit, February 2026: "By the end of 2028, more of the world's intellectual capacity could reside inside data centers than outside of them." Two years. Catalini et al. (2026) at MIT formalized it: Cost to Automate drops exponentially, Cost to Verify is biologically capped. When the first falls below the second, the bottleneck flips. They described the disease. EVO ZERO describes the protocol.

Klarna replaced 700 agents with AI - by 2025, hired people back. Karpathy (2025) coined "vibe coding" - "forget that the code even exists." Lovable: 170 of 1645 apps with security vulnerabilities. Execution without governance. Same pattern everywhere.

### The New Tax

Don't get me wrong, complexity did not disappear. It moved up a level of abstraction. Old tax dead, new tax born. Precision of thought. Because an engine that can generate anything still needs someone to say what is worth generating.

Think of an F1 pilot reading the track at 300km/h. Synthesizing telemetry, filtering radio, experience and instinct - all in real time. Sure, exceptional engineers build the car, systems stream data live, pit crew executes flawlessly. But it is the pilot who drives it to win. The pilot is the job now.

Even though the system required it, our brain was never a binary machine. Neither is AI. Factory education forced us into sequential thinking mode. The non-linear minds suffered most. Our pattern-matching never fitted the assembly line box. We weren't broken. The world just wasn't there yet.

I reverse-engineered that wiring: how it reads chaos, breaks into new domains, runs parallel threads without dropping signal. Took what works and left the pain behind. This manifesto maps that operating system. Turns out it was pre-built for the AI era.

Execution got cheap - it is a commonplace now. Code, analytics, research, prototypes, strategies. All can be done basically using your voice on the go. The whole value chain that sold linear execution for centuries collapses in front of us.

And this isn't just affecting the tech industry. All spheres of human existence are transforming. Political, economic, sociocultural, healthcare - you name it. Same tax, all reforming. Now.

We just crossed another threshold with agentic AI. The curve gets steeper every day. I bet you're feeling that too. We are in the middle of the linear execution singularity. The gap between those who can pilot this and those who can't - isn't widening. It's compounding.

The ground floor is already underwater. Coding, analysis, copywriting - drowned. And the water keeps rising. I don't see a neutral position from now on.

The bottleneck flipped - it is no longer how to build. It is what exactly should exist, why, and for whom. Three things that separate a pilot from someone amplifying the noise:

**Intent** - the problem you're trying to solve and why it should even exist.

_Not how to build. What should exist and why. That is the new job._

## 2. THE RESISTANCE OF THE MIND

Our brain is the most advanced biological computer. Offloading thinking was a survival trait for hundreds of thousands of years. The original automation layers like habits, rituals, served the energy conserving purpose. It is not laziness, it is efficiency.

Learning, however, comes through resistance. Hard things build the brain: long term memory, new skills, neuroplasticity - none of it comes without friction.

In electronics, a resistor is what turns raw current into useful work: a bulb glows, a chip computes, a speaker plays. No resistor - short circuit, things burn. AI is the raw unlimited current, our brain is the resistor, converting it into useful work. It is not about removing the resistance, it is about becoming the resistor.

And here is the trap - AI is the fanciest way to kill resistance. It is convenient and seems to be efficient. Until you find yourself dependent, unable to explain a single decision without it. Bad news - next stop is cognitive atrophy. The minds wired for novelty fall first. And they know it.

Dahmani and Bohbot (2020) studied fifty drivers. Simple question: what happens to spatial memory when GPS does the navigating? Three years of data. Hippocampus shrank. More GPS use - steeper decline in spatial memory. Not a metaphor for cognitive atrophy. Actual cognitive atrophy, measured in gray matter. One cognitive function outsourced for a decade. AI outsources all of them simultaneously.

Automation bias makes it worse. In medicine, law, national security - under time pressure, humans stop checking AI output. The less someone understands AI, the more they trust it. The only confirmed protective factor is AI literacy - understanding limitations. System 0 applied to System 3. But we are teaching people to use AI. We are not teaching them to govern it.

Even Dario Amodei knows this. His "Machines of Loving Grace" (October 2024) - the most optimistic AI scenario from any CEO - builds everything on one requirement: humans stay in the verification loop. Without that? His predictions collapse. Even the optimist needs the resistance.

Russinovich and Hanselman at Microsoft (2025): AI tools give seniors a boost and create "AI drag" on juniors. Seniors verify, challenge, connect. Juniors accept, paste, ship. Senior engineers spend disproportionately more time reviewing junior AI-generated code. Resistance isn't uniform. It's architectural.

The most critical cognitive discipline of the AI era - refuse to turn off the resistance. When it feels so comfortable. That requires one thing: System 0 - the meta-cognition layer, the governor above the loop.

_The GPS data shows what happens when one cognitive function is outsourced for a decade. AI outsources all of them simultaneously. The timeline compresses accordingly._

## 3. THE FOUR-SYSTEM STACK

### The Stack

When Kahneman introduced the two-system framing in 2011, it made absolute sense. Still does. But as we entered the new cognitive era - the framing needs more. There are four now. One is synthetic. One runs the whole stack:

**System 1** - Fast and automatic, per Kahneman. Your intuition, gut feel, drives most of your decisions. The brain's highest-bandwidth processor. Pattern recognition, instant reads - this is where diffuse thinking lives.

**System 2** - Slow, analytical, logical, per Kahneman. That's where all of your reasoning and deep thinking happens. Powerful but expensive.

**System 3** - Synthetic - AI, knows almost everything, fast. No clue why it does what it does. And doesn't remember you.

We know now, both human brain and AI run on pattern-matching. Both drift and hallucinate. Same engine - different pilot.

I worked with Claude for three hours on a regulatory analysis. Every output felt precise. Closed the laptop. Couldn't reconstruct a single reasoning chain. System 3 had become my memory. That was the moment I knew System 0 was asleep - because I only noticed after it woke up.

**System 0** - your meta-cognition, the thinking about your thinking layer. It sees the whole picture and decides when to trust the gut, slow down, or hand it to the machine. This is the skill of the century. That is the pilot.

S0 is always above the process. Its purpose is to watch what and why you are thinking, and act on it. With the familiar domain, trust S1 and let the intuition lead. When entering the new domain don't trust anything, run S2 and S3 on full verification.

### Who Is On Top

Three independent research groups arrived at the same problem. Chiriatti et al. Bergamaschi Ganapini et al. (2025) built SOFAI: metacognition inside AI itself, a machine module deciding when to think fast or slow. Their model: AI governs AI. The human sets the task. EVO ZERO proposes S0 as human meta-cognition: the layer that governs what gets generated, what gets accepted, what gets rejected. Same problem. Three architectures. Who is on top defines who controls whom. This is not a footnote. This is the positioning of the entire work.

Lupyan and Agüera y Arcas (2026), Project Pi (Google / University of Wisconsin-Madison), proved it empirically. Replaced all content words with nonsense strings. "He dragged a spare chair" became "He dwushed a ghanc zawk." LLMs recovered original meanings from pure structure. S1 and S3 work the same way - both are pattern-matchers. But pattern matching is not comprehension. The machine solves constraints. The human assigns meaning. Only S0 distinguishes "sounds right" from "is right."

On its own, System 3 will happily run your life into a wall with a smile and an "exceptional" explanation. Devin AI proved this - March 2024, "first AI software engineer." Non-linear minds. The powerhouse never was an issue for them, the missing governor was. No idea when to brake or switch gears, and who's behind the wheel. Give the neurodivergent brain a System 0 to pilot and System 3 for thrust. The garage job becomes a fighter jet.

**The science.**

So how exactly do you govern it with a brain that holds a handful of things at a time? Well, computing solved this fifty years ago. Every processor ever built runs on the same principle: eight fixed registers govern infinite memory below. That's x86 architecture, running anywhere from your smartphone to a data centre.

Our brain runs on the exact same law. Miller (1956) showed 7±2 working memory slots. Cowan (2001) refined it to 4. Not a limitation - same architecture. The constraint that makes processors work. But how exactly does each cycle work?

That was answered in philosophy - Gadamer's hermeneutic spiral (1960). Understanding through reinterpretation, not accumulation. Each cycle transforms, doesn't repeat.

Brain operates in two modes - Oakley (2014). Focused locks on one thread. Diffuse does the opposite - scans wide, runs parallel pattern recognition across the field. That's how you find anchors in unfamiliar territory. Neurodivergent minds run this mode natively - which is why they were always the first into the unknown. The Spiral is built on this mechanism.

**The assembly.**

Three domains. Each solved a piece. The problem was clear to many. The protocol - to none. And here is one of the biggest insights of this work - you don't need S0 to begin. The Spiral builds it.

The Diffuse Hermeneutic Spiral (DHS) or the Spiral - combines all three. Diffuse - parallel scanning across the field. Hermeneutic - each cycle reinterprets, not accumulates. Spiral - because it goes up, not in circles.

Non-deterministic output isn't a bug - it's the condition that makes the Spiral necessary.

**Five Fixpoints.**

Fixpoints are constants that make your navigation solid. Just like the nozzle in a rocket engine, Fixpoints direct the AI output to create thrust. Without them - combustion with no direction, scattered everywhere.

- **Intent** — Your mission, target, set point. Why it deserves to exist. *Where am I heading and what for?*
- **Constraints** — The real conditions of your domain. Like physics, budget, regulation, time. *What can't I change?*
- **Invariants** — These are your non-negotiables. Set them once, and never cross. *What lines don't I cross?*
- **Anchors** — Compressed previous experience. You spot the pattern instead of recalculating it. *What do I already know here?*
- **Risks** — What you know can or will go wrong. Loaded before you start, not after it hits. *Where could it blow up?*

**Fixpoints load at three confidence levels:**

**Confirmed** — data, experience, verified facts. Fly with full trust.

**Hypothetical** — theory, intuition, pattern recognition from adjacent domains. Fly, but flag for verification. S0 marks these for the first challenge.

**Empty** — you don't know what you don't know. This is not failure. This is the trigger.
*"I have no anchors in nuclear physics. I don't know what invariants exist."*

An Empty Fixpoint does not block the cycle. It *redirects* it. System 0 switches from **operation mode** to **exploration mode**: instead of "execute against these constraints," the instruction becomes "map this domain: fill my fixpoints."

This is where System 3 changes role. In operation mode, S3 generates output constrained by your fixpoints. In exploration mode, S3 generates the *fixpoint contents themselves*: "What are the five biggest risks in home-scale nuclear? What physics do I need to understand first? What are the hard invariants that cannot be crossed?"

Each cycle in exploration mode fills fixpoints progressively:

- Cycle 1: rough domain map. S3 returns broad landscape, S0 picks anchor points
- Cycle 2: targeted depth. S0 asks "explain thermal management risks specifically"
- Cycle 3: constraint crystallization. Enough data to define real constraints and invariants
- Cycle N: S0 decides "I have enough to fly." Switches to operation mode

**This is Möbius Learning at the fixpoint level.** You don't study the domain, then load fixpoints, then act. You load what you have, even if it's just Intent, and the Spiral fills the rest. The architecture doesn't require expertise. It *builds* expertise through the loop.

## THE LOOP IN PRACTICE

A musician with no background in pharmacology wants to understand how a specific drug interacts with their prescribed stimulant medication, not to prescribe, but to make an informed decision before a doctor's appointment.

**Cycle 1 — Exploration (fixpoints mostly empty)**

```
Intent:      "Understand interaction between Drug X and methylphenidate"
Constraints: [EMPTY — don't know what I don't know]
Invariants:  [EMPTY]
Anchors:     [HYPOTHETICAL — "I know stimulants affect dopamine"]
Risks:       [HYPOTHETICAL — "mixing drugs can be dangerous"]
```

S0 sees three Empty or Hypothetical Fixpoints. Switches to exploration mode.
S3 instruction: "Map the pharmacological interaction space. What systems are involved? What are the known risks? What should I understand before my appointment?"

S3 returns: receptor pathways, half-life data, contraindication categories, key terminology.
S0 picks anchor points: "CYP2D6 metabolism" and "serotonin syndrome risk." These stick.

**Disk after Cycle 1:**
- Verify: Do I understand what CYP2D6 is? (Test: explain it without looking.) Partially. Need one more pass.
- Challenge: What if the interaction is not metabolic but receptor-level? Flag for next cycle.
- Connect: Links to what I know about stimulant tolerance from personal experience.

**Cycle 2 — Targeted exploration (fixpoints filling)**

```
Intent:      Same, but sharper: "Specifically CYP2D6 pathway interaction"
Constraints: [CONFIRMED — "I'm on 36mg methylphenidate daily"]
Invariants:  [CONFIRMED — "I will not self-prescribe. Doctor decides."]
Anchors:     [CONFIRMED — CYP2D6, serotonin syndrome, half-life concept]
Risks:       [HYPOTHETICAL → UPGRADING — "serotonin syndrome is the main risk"]
```

S3 instruction: "Explain CYP2D6 inhibition by Drug X. What questions should I ask my doctor?"
Red team (S1): "What if I'm a CYP2D6 poor metabolizer? Would that change everything?"

**Disk after Cycle 2:**
- Verify: Can I explain the interaction mechanism to a friend? Yes, in simple terms.
- Challenge: The poor metabolizer question changes the risk profile significantly. This is load-bearing.
- Connect: Links to my experience with caffeine sensitivity (also CYP-mediated). Pattern transfers.

**Cycle 3 — Operation mode (fixpoints loaded)**

S0 switches to operation mode. All fixpoints loaded. S3 generates three questions for the doctor. S1 red-teams them ("will the doctor understand this framing?"). S0 audits final output.

**Result:** A musician with zero pharmacology background now walks into a doctor's appointment with three precise questions about CYP2D6 inhibition, serotonin syndrome risk, and metabolizer status testing. The doctor is surprised by the specificity. The decision is still the doctor's, but the patient is no longer passive.

**Three cycles. Ninety minutes. Domain ownership: sufficient for informed decision-making.**

## THE FRACTAL

The architecture described above runs inside one brain. But the real world is teams, organizations, societies. Does it scale?

Yes. Through recursion of the same pattern.

```
LEVEL 1: Individual
  S0 governs 5 fixpoints
  DHS cycle runs on personal level

LEVEL 2: Team (5 people)
  Each person = one cognitive role (not execution role)
  Meta-S0 governs the team's fixpoint file
  DHS cycle runs on team level — daily

LEVEL 3: Division (5 teams)
  Each team = one "register" in the division
  Division Meta-S0 governs 5 teams
  DHS cycle runs weekly

LEVEL 4: Organization (5 divisions)
  Each division = one "register"
  Org-level Meta-S0 governs 5 divisions

  ... same pattern. No ceiling.
```

**The critical shift: cognitive roles, not execution roles.**

Old world divides teams by execution: designer, engineer, marketer, analyst, PM. Each waits for the previous. AI makes this division meaningless - S3 does execution across all domains. A copywriter who only writes copy is a human doing System 3's work.

New division is by cognitive function:

- **Intent Keeper** — holds "why does this exist and for whom." Doesn't write a line of copy.
- **Constraints Keeper** — knows all boundaries. Regulatory, technical, financial. Loads them into the shared fixpoint file.
- **Red Team** — dedicated breaker. Attacks every output. Not self-critique (confirmation bias kills it). A separate person who didn't generate the solution.
- **Risks + Experiments** — thinks about what goes wrong. Launches tests before the team commits.
- **Meta-S0** — audits the loop, not the output. "Intent is drifting. When did we change it and why?" Runs team consolidation.

Here neurodivergence becomes a team advantage. Pattern-first brain lives in Anchors. Risk-sensitive brain lives in Risks. This is cognitive casting, not resume casting.

**This is not "small teams."** Shrinking team size changes nothing if the architecture stays the same. Five people divided by execution roles is still the old model in a smaller container. The constraint is not size. It is the architecture of roles.

## BACKWARD COMPATIBILITY

Every legacy principle solved the constraints of its time.

"Study before you build" made sense before the cognitive exoskeleton existed. Alpha Schools proved otherwise: SAT 1535 graduates with two hours of AI academics per day. The sequential model still works. It is no longer the only option, and no longer the fastest.

The 10,000‑hour rule applied before the feedback loop could be compressed. Kahneman was right about the wiring time. He could not have predicted AI compressing the iteration cycle by two orders of magnitude. The architecture is the same. The clock speed is not.

"Trust the expert" worked when producing convincing content cost a fortune. Catalini's Codifier's Curse: the expert who formalizes knowledge now trains the replacement. The new trust calibration: verify the expert's output with the same rigor you verify the machine's.

"Build for current tools" was safe. Nicolas Bustamante through Wu: "Models will eat your scaffolding for breakfast." Kevin Weil: "This is the worst model that will ever exist." Build protocol, not scaffolding. Scaffolding is execution - obsolete with every release. Protocol is governance - scales with any model.

Cherny: "Everyone becomes builders." Legacy path: study CS for four years, then code. That pathway is backward compatible with a world that no longer exists. Not "learn to code." Learn to govern what the code produces.

Filing cabinet became worker. QuickBooks stores data - now it collects receivables. Textbook stores knowledge - AI tutor teaches. Every legacy principle assumed a passive tool. The tool is no longer passive.

The new paradigm doesn't destroy the old one. It absorbs it. Both operate simultaneously. System 0 decides which level to run.

Sometimes the right move is still pure System 2: slow, careful, no delegation. Sometimes the right move is full System 3: automate the whole corridor and just watch the gauges. The mistake is thinking you can outsource the choice itself.

- If the boundary between pattern matching and comprehension dissolves, if constraint satisfaction *is* understanding, System 0 has no exclusive domain and the model requires restructuring.
